# The Planck Horizon

**The Planck Horizon (TPH)** is QPlanck's quantum intelligence newsletter. It is
designed to interpret consequential developments in quantum computing, quantum
physics, and deep technology—not simply summarize the news cycle.

Its editorial promise is **signal, not noise**: explain the underlying mechanism,
identify what changes because of it, and make the longer-term implications clear.

## Audience

TPH is written for readers who want technically grounded interpretation,
including:

- quantum researchers and engineers;
- deep-tech founders and operators;
- physics-literate investors;
- students building serious domain knowledge; and
- readers interested in the frontier of computation.

## Issue structure

Each issue follows six sections:

1. **Signal** — the highest-signal development and why it matters.
2. **Deep Dive** — a paper, concept, or company explained from first principles.
3. **What This Unlocks** — practical implications and new directions.
4. **Second-Order Effects** — downstream consequences, winners, and risks.
5. **QPlanck Insight** — a clear, non-obvious editorial perspective.
6. **Underrated Opportunity** — an overlooked research or business opportunity.

## Relationship to QPlanck

TPH is QPlanck's interpretation and publishing layer. It can make complex work
more legible and surface important questions, while QCore and QPlanck Labs focus
on software, experimentation, and learning infrastructure.

Editorial analysis must remain distinguishable from verified product
capabilities and formal research results elsewhere in this documentation.
