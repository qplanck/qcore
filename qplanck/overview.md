# What is QPlanck?

QPlanck is a quantum technology organization developing software, research and
learning infrastructure, and high-signal technical media. Its work is organized
around a simple idea: progress in quantum technology needs better tools and
better understanding at the same time.

## The QPlanck ecosystem

QPlanck's divisions serve different parts of that mission:

- **QCore** develops open-source quantum software and interoperability tooling.
- **QPlanck Labs** turns technical concepts into practical, reproducible learning
  and research experiences.
- **The Planck Horizon** interprets important developments for a technically
  literate audience.

These divisions are related, but their claims and maturity levels remain
separate. A roadmap item in QCore, for example, is not presented as an available
feature, and an editorial thesis in The Planck Horizon is not presented as a
technical product guarantee.

## Documentation principles

This hub records:

- what exists now;
- what has been proposed;
- the evidence behind important decisions;
- how QPlanck's divisions relate to one another; and
- how contributors and readers can participate.

New divisions can be added without turning their early ideas into established
capabilities. Their documentation should state ownership, maturity, evidence,
and scope explicitly.
